**Why matters**: Birth plan communicates your preferences to hospital staff quickly, especially important if there's a shift change during labor. Having preferences documented helps ensure your wishes are respected while remaining flexible for medical needs.

**Done when**: Birth preferences worksheet completed, discussed with care team, and 3-4 printed copies added to hospital bag.

## Key Topics to Address

### Atmosphere Preferences
- Lighting (soft/dim preferred?)
- Music (bring speaker and playlist?)
- Visitors (who and when?)
- Photography/video

### Labor Preferences
- Freedom of movement
- Eating/drinking during labor
- Use of birth ball, shower, tub
- Intermittent vs. continuous fetal monitoring

### Pain Management
- Natural labor techniques preferred
- Open to pain medication if needed
- Specific preferences: nitrous oxide, analgesic, epidural
- When to offer pain relief options

### Delivery Preferences
- Preferred pushing positions
- Mirror to watch delivery?
- Partner cutting umbilical cord?
- Skin-to-skin immediately after birth
- Delayed cord clamping

### Postpartum/Baby Care
- Immediate skin-to-skin (at least 1 hour)
- Breastfeeding initiation timing
- Vitamin K shot, eye ointment, Hepatitis B vaccine
- Circumcision preferences (if applicable)
- Rooming-in vs. nursery care

## BRAIN Decision Framework

Use BRAIN method (learned in Childbirth Intensive) for any medical decisions during labor:

- **B**enefits - What are the benefits?
- **R**isks - What are the risks?
- **A**lternatives - What other options exist?
- **I**ntuition - What does your gut say?
- **N**o/Not Now - Can we wait?
