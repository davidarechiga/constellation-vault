**Why matters**: Birth videos help set realistic expectations, show comfort measures in action, normalize the sights and sounds of labor, and reduce stress by building familiarity with the birthing process. Research shows that visual preparation helps parents feel more confident and less anxious on the big day.

**Done when**: Both David & Camille have watched at least 3-5 birth videos from different sources, discussed takeaways together, and feel more prepared for what to expect during labor.

## Recommended Video Resources

### Educational & Evidence-Based
- **[University of Minnesota School of Nursing - Labor & Birth Videos](https://nursing.umn.edu/academics/specialty-areas/nurse-midwifery/having-baby-prenatal-videos/having-baby-labor-and-birth)**
  - Professional educational videos about what to expect during labor and birth

- **[Global Health Media Project - Childbirth Series](https://globalhealthmedia.org/our-projects/childbirth-series/)**
  - Evidence-based videos covering normal birth, midwifery skills, and complications
  - Demonstrates best-practice care during labor, birth, and immediate postpartum

### Realistic & Positive Birth Stories
- **[The Positive Birth Company - Giving Birth Videos](https://thepositivebirthcompany.com/giving-birth-videos)**
  - Raw, realistic videos showing the strength and beauty of different birthing experiences
  - Various techniques and approaches

- **[Mama Natural - Delivery Videos](https://www.mamanatural.com/delivery-videos/)**
  - Natural birth videos showing what delivery is really like

- **[She Births Bravely - 11 Real + Positive Birth Videos](https://shebirthsbravely.com/10-birth-videos-every-pregnant-mama-should-watch/)**
  - Empowering, positive birth stories

- **[Care.com - 15 Birth Videos to Prepare You](https://www.care.com/c/12-baby-birth-videos-that-really-prepare-yo/)**
  - Comprehensive collection showing various birth experiences

### What to Look For While Watching
- **Comfort measures**: Positions, breathing, movement, partner support
- **Labor progression**: What early vs. active labor vs. transition looks like
- **Sounds**: Moaning, vocalizations (totally normal!)
- **Support**: How partners and doulas provide encouragement
- **Different experiences**: Hospital vs. home, medicated vs. unmedicated, calm vs. intense
- **Immediate postpartum**: Skin-to-skin, first moments, placenta delivery

### Discussion Questions After Watching
- What surprised you?
- Which comfort measures looked most helpful?
- What would you like to incorporate into your birth plan?
- Any concerns or questions that came up?
- What role feels right for David as birth partner?
