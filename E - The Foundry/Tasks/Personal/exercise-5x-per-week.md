## Exercise Options

**Gym Workouts** (Primary):
- Strength training with weights
- Cardio machines (treadmill, bike, elliptical)
- Circuit training
- Duration: 45-60 minutes

**Bike Rides** (Alternative):
- Morning rides (7am)
- Evening rides with friends
- Weekend longer rides
- Duration: 30-45 minutes

**Stretching / Physical Therapy**:
- Morning stretching routine
- Physical therapy exercises for recovery
- Yoga or mobility work
- Duration: 20-30 minutes

**Dog Walks with River**:
- Brisk-paced walks (can count as light exercise)
- Training walks with high energy
- Duration: 20-30 minutes

**Why matters:** Regular exercise supports physical and mental health, reduces stress, improves sleep quality, and builds energy for upcoming parenthood demands. Maintaining fitness habit before baby arrives makes it easier to return to exercise postpartum.

**Done when:** 5 workout sessions completed in a 7-day week, tracked and logged.

## Tips for Consistency

✅ **Success Strategies:**
- **Pre-plan workout week** - Decide Sunday which days you'll exercise
- **Morning workouts** - Get it done before day gets busy (especially with baby coming)
- **Workout buddy** - Accountability through friend or Camille
- **Track visually** - Seeing checkboxes fill in is motivating
- **Flexible mindset** - 20 min is better than 0 min
- **Prepare gear** - Gym clothes laid out night before

❌ **Common Pitfalls:**
- All-or-nothing thinking ("If I can't do full workout, I won't do any")
- Skipping when tired (movement often creates energy)
- No rest days (leads to burnout)
- Comparing to past fitness (focus on current consistency)

---

## Adaptation for New Parent Life

**After baby arrives:**
- Lower target to 3x/week minimum
- Shorter sessions okay (20-30 min counts)
- Home workouts or walks with stroller
- Exercise when baby naps
- Be gentle with yourself - sleep is also important

**Goal:** Maintain habit through major life transition, even if intensity decreases.
