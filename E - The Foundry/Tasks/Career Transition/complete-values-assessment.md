# Complete Values/Career Assessment

- [ ] Set aside 30-60 minutes for reflection
- [ ] Review current career dissatisfactions (what to avoid)
- [ ] Identify core values (work-life balance, creativity, autonomy, growth, etc.)
- [ ] Assess technical skills and strengths
- [ ] Assess design skills and creative strengths
- [ ] Define ideal work environment (remote, hybrid, in-person)
- [ ] Clarify non-negotiables (salary range, flexibility, culture)
- [ ] Document findings in career notes

**Why matters**: Clear understanding of values and priorities guides job search strategy. With baby arriving soon, work-life balance and flexibility are especially critical. This assessment prevents accepting wrong-fit roles.

**Done when**: Career values document completed with priorities ranked, non-negotiables identified, and ideal role characteristics defined.

**Related**: [[Career Transition 2026]] • [[Career Development Hub]]

---

## Key Questions to Consider

### Work-Life Balance
- What does work-life balance mean with a newborn?
- How important is remote work vs. in-office?
- What flexibility do you need (hours, location, parental leave)?
- How much travel is acceptable?

### Creative + Technical Balance
- What ratio of design vs. development work feels ideal?
- Do you want to specialize or stay generalist?
- How important is working with cutting-edge technologies?
- Do you prefer building new things or improving existing products?

### Team & Culture
- What size company appeals to you? (Startup, mid-size, enterprise)
- Do you want to be individual contributor or move toward management?
- How important is mentorship/learning opportunities?
- What kind of team collaboration energizes you?

### Growth & Learning
- What skills do you want to develop next?
- How important is formal training/education budget?
- Do you want to work on products that make an impact?
- What industries interest you?

### Compensation & Benefits
- Minimum salary requirement: $___________
- Target salary range: $___________
- Benefits priorities: (health insurance, 401k, PTO, parental leave, etc.)
- Equity/stock options important?

## Output Format

Create a simple document with:
1. **Top 5 Values** (ranked)
2. **Non-Negotiables** (deal-breakers)
3. **Nice-to-Haves** (preferences but flexible)
4. **Red Flags** (warning signs to avoid)
5. **Ideal Role Description** (1-2 paragraphs)

This becomes your compass for evaluating opportunities.
