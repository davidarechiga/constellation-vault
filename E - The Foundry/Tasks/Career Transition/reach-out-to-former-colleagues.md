# Reach Out to Former Colleagues

## Identify Contacts
- [ ] Make list of 10-15 former colleagues, managers, collaborators
- [ ] Prioritize those in relevant industries (tech, design, creative)
- [ ] Identify who might know Creative Technologist opportunities
- [ ] Note relationship context (when/where you worked together)

## Craft Outreach Messages
- [ ] Write personalized message template
- [ ] Mention specific shared experience or project
- [ ] Explain career transition (Creative Technologist focus)
- [ ] Ask for advice, not directly for job
- [ ] Keep message concise (5-7 sentences max)

## Send Outreach
- [ ] Send 2-3 messages per day (avoid mass blast)
- [ ] Personalize each message (no copy-paste)
- [ ] Use LinkedIn, email, or text (choose based on relationship)
- [ ] Track who you contacted and when
- [ ] Follow up if no response after 1 week

## Engage & Follow Up
- [ ] Respond promptly to replies (within 24 hours)
- [ ] Schedule coffee chats or calls if offered
- [ ] Share updated LinkedIn profile when asked
- [ ] Offer value in return (help with their projects, intros, etc.)
- [ ] Send thank you notes after conversations

**Why matters**: Warm introductions through former colleagues are most effective way to find opportunities. Networking opens doors that cold applications cannot. Personal connections lead to referrals, insider info, and faster hiring processes.

**Done when**: 10-15 former colleagues contacted, conversations scheduled with those who respond, and thank you follow-ups sent.

## Outreach Message Template

**Subject**: Catching up + Career advice

**Message**:
> Hi [Name],
>
> Hope you're doing well! I've been thinking about [specific project/memory from working together] and wanted to reconnect.
>
> I'm currently exploring a transition into Creative Technologist roles (design + dev hybrid work) and would love to get your perspective on the market. Your experience at [their company] would be incredibly valuable as I think about next steps.
>
> Would you have 15-20 minutes for a quick coffee/call in the next couple weeks? Happy to work around your schedule.
>
> Either way, hope things are going great for you!
>
> Best,
> David

## Conversation Goals

When connecting with former colleagues, aim to:

1. **Reconnect genuinely** - Catch up on their life and work
2. **Share your transition** - Explain Creative Technologist goal clearly
3. **Ask for advice** - "What should I know about this role/market?"
4. **Learn about their company** - Are they hiring? What's the culture like?
5. **Request introductions** - "Do you know anyone in this space I should talk to?"

**Don't ask directly for a job** - Let them offer if appropriate.

## Who to Contact

Prioritize people who:
- Work at companies you're interested in
- Are in design or engineering leadership
- Have hired Creative Technologists or similar roles
- Know your work quality and can vouch for skills
- Are active in tech/design communities

---

## Conversation Tips

**DO**:
- Be genuine and authentic
- Ask about their current work
- Listen more than you talk
- Offer help if you can
- Express gratitude for their time

**DON'T**:
- Launch directly into asking for favors
- Complain about current/past jobs
- Only talk about yourself
- Be pushy about job opportunities
- Forget to follow up with thank you
