### Deep Cleaning Rotation

## Before Baby Arrival

**Target completion: February 22, 2026**

### Kitchen Deep Clean
  - Clean inside microwave, oven, and refrigerator
  - Wipe down all cabinet fronts and handles
  - Organize pantry and discard expired items
  - Deep clean sink and faucet (remove hard water stains)
  - Mop floor and baseboards
  - **Done when**: Kitchen is guest-ready and fully sanitized for baby prep

### Bathroom Deep Clean
  - Scrub tub, shower, and tile grout
  - Clean toilet thoroughly (including behind and under)
  - Wipe down all surfaces, mirrors, and fixtures
  - Organize under-sink storage
  - Wash bath mats and shower curtain
  - Restock toiletries and supplies
  - **Done when**: Bathroom is spotless and ready for postpartum recovery

### Dining Room Deep Clean
  - Dust light fixture and ceiling fan
  - Clean table and chairs thoroughly
  - Vacuum or mop floor
  - Wipe down baseboards and window sills
  - Organize any clutter or storage items
  - **Done when**: Space is clear and welcoming for family meals

### Entry Way Deep Clean
  - Organize shoe storage
  - Clean front door and hardware
  - Sweep/mop floor
  - Dust any decorative items
  - Create designated spot for diaper bag and baby gear
  - **Done when**: Entryway is organized and ready for quick exits with baby

## Notes

**Purpose**: These routines help maintain a clean, organized home environment as we prepare for Cyrus's arrival and adjust to life with a newborn.

**Priority Adjustment**: Deep cleaning tasks are high priority before baby arrives, then shift to maintenance mode afterward.

## Fair Play Cards: Household

> I own the full CPE for all these cards — not just execution, but noticing and planning too.

### Daily Cards

**Dishes**
- **C:** Notice when dishes pile up; see what needs hand-washing vs. dishwasher
- **P:** Anticipate load on heavy cooking days; keep soap/sponge stocked
- **E:** Load, run, unload; hand-wash as needed; wipe sink
- **MSC:** Sink is clear before bed. Dishwasher never left full and clean overnight.

**Tidying Up / Organizing**
- **C:** Notice when spaces feel cluttered; identify donation candidates
- **P:** Schedule tidy sessions; plan donation runs
- **E:** Put things away, declutter, bag donations, drop at donation center
- **MSC:** Common areas reset daily. Donation bags gone within 2 weeks of filling.

**Mail**
- **C:** Notice when mail accumulates; identify action items vs. junk
- **P:** Sort daily; decide what to file, shred, act on, or pay
- **E:** Open, sort, file, pay, shred same day
- **MSC:** No mail sits unopened 24+ hours. Paper doesn't pile on counters.

### Weekly Cards

**Laundry**
- **C:** Notice laundry levels household-wide; track what's running low
- **P:** Schedule wash days; keep supplies stocked; know what needs special care
- **E:** Wash, dry, fold, put away; restock supplies
- **MSC:** Nothing sits in dryer more than a day. Folded and put away (not piled). No one runs out of basics.

**Meals (Weekend)**
- **C:** Think about what we're eating Sat/Sun; check fridge and pantry
- **P:** Plan meals by Friday evening; shop or order if needed
- **E:** Cook, serve, clean up
- **MSC:** Weekend plan discussed with Camille before Saturday morning. At least one intentional shared meal per weekend.

**Weekend Plans**
- **C:** Think ahead — what does the family need? Rest? Activity? Errands?
- **P:** Propose weekend plan to Camille by Thursday
- **E:** Book/arrange activities; manage day-of logistics
- **MSC:** Plan discussed before Saturday morning. At least one fun or restorative thing per weekend.

### As-Needed Cards

**Garbage & Recycling** — bins never overflowing; out the night before pickup (Wednesday)

**Dry Cleaning** — items don't sit in "needs dry cleaning" pile more than 2 weeks

**Home Furnishings & Décor** — broken/needed items researched thoughtfully; not impulse-bought

**Storage / Garage & Seasonal Items** — storage organized enough to find items in 5 min; seasonal swap within 2 weeks of season change

**Returns & Store Credits** — no return window missed; items out of the house within 2 weeks

**Electronics & IT** — Wi-Fi functional; tech issues resolved within a week; subscriptions audited

**Auto** — always registered + insured; oil changes on schedule; warning lights addressed within a week

**Transportation (Kids)** — Cyrus never late due to poor planning; car seat always installed; logistics coordinated in advance

### Financial Cards

**Money Manager**
- **C:** Know the full financial picture — income, bills, subscriptions, debt, savings
- **P:** Track budget; schedule bill payments; plan for big expenses
- **E:** Pay bills on time; reconcile accounts; handle financial paperwork
- **MSC:** No bills late. Budget reviewed monthly. No surprise overdrafts. Both partners have financial awareness.

**Cash & Bills** — No bill paid late. Autopay set up for recurring bills. Cash/petty expenses tracked.

**Health Insurance** — No claim unsubmitted. Open enrollment handled before deadline. Both partners know coverage.

### Annual / Seasonal Cards

**Holidays** — No holiday arrives as a surprise. Gifts purchased before the week of. Traditions protected and intentional.

**Birthday Celebrations (Cyrus)** — Planning starts 4–6 weeks out. Cyrus feels special on his birthday.

**Parents & In-Laws** — Regular check-ins at least monthly per family. Major events acknowledged proactively.
