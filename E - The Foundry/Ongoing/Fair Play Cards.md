## **All Fair Play Cards** — all 100, grouped by category:

**Magic**
1. **Adult Friendships (Player 1)** — Each of you gets this card because friends are key to your health, happiness, resilience, career, and sanity. Whether it's grabbing coffee after school drop-off, a dinner date, a networking event, or a weekend trip away, nurturing friendships will reap huge dividends. Friendships are not a luxury you'll make time for in the future so use this card guilt-free now. No partner resentment allowed either, as a girls' or guys' night out is about so much more than a cocktail. It's about cultivating relationships that will support and sustain you along with boosting the health of your marriage.

2. **Adult Friendships (Player 2)** — [Same description as Player 1 — both partners hold this card.]

3. **Aging/Ailing Parent** — When a parent or in-law needs you, it can feel like a second job. Whether you're driving to doctor's appointments, handling insurance claims, researching living facilities, or simply offering emotional support, caring for an aging parent takes time, energy, and planning that must be acknowledged and distributed fairly between partners.

4. **Bedtime Routine (Kids)** — Whoever holds this card owns the "getting kids to sleep" marathon. That includes all the steps: bath, pajamas, brushing teeth, story time, lullabies, water requests, monster checks, and the final tuck-in. It's nightly, relentless, and deeply loving work.

5. **Daily Grinds** — These are the micro-tasks that keep a household running on a day-to-day basis but don't fit neatly into other cards — things like wiping down the counter, replacing the toilet paper roll, or turning off the lights. Small but endless.

6. **Family Fun** — This cardholder plans and executes leisure activities and fun experiences for the family — from weekend outings to vacation ideas to figuring out what to do on a rainy afternoon. It goes beyond showing up; it's about making the magic happen.

7. **Grooming (Kids)** — Haircuts, nail trimming, dental hygiene coaching, bathing schedules — this cardholder keeps the kids clean, groomed, and presentable. That means scheduling the appointments, showing up, and managing the drama that often follows.

8. **Hard Conversations with Kids** — Sex ed, death, racism, drugs, divorce — whoever holds this card owns those tough but essential talks. It requires knowing what to say, when to say it, and how to follow up.

9. **Homework & School Projects** — This cardholder is the homework enforcer and school project manager — overseeing the nightly grind, science fair disasters, and last-minute poster boards. It includes tracking what's due, when it's due, and making sure it gets done.

10. **Informal Education (Kids)** — Do you remember who taught you how to hold your breath underwater? Throw a ball? Ride a bike? This cardholder owns those milestone teaching moments and life skills — from tying shoes to swimming to driving.

11. **Magical Beings** — Santa, the Tooth Fairy, the Easter Bunny — this cardholder keeps the magic alive. That means remembering, planning, and executing the little rituals that make childhood memorable.

12. **Marriage & Partnership** — This cardholder takes the lead in actively investing in the relationship — planning date nights, initiating connection, noticing when things feel off, and doing something about it.

13. **Medium of Record** — Whoever holds this card decides how family history is documented and stored — photos organized, videos backed up, albums or scrapbooks created. The memories don't preserve themselves.

14. **Memories & Photos** — This cardholder captures, curates, and preserves family moments — from everyday snapshots to big milestones. Includes printing photos, making albums, and keeping digital archives organized.

15. **Spirituality & Religion** — Whether it's choosing a faith community, maintaining religious traditions, or simply cultivating a sense of wonder and meaning in the household, this cardholder leads that effort.

16. **Thank You Notes & Correspondence** — Gifts get acknowledged, RSVPs get sent, sympathy cards get mailed. The social glue of the household — this cardholder manages it.

17. **Unicorn Space (Player 1)** — Your Unicorn Space is the pursuit that makes you uniquely you. This card is permission — and a reminder — to protect time for whatever lights you up creatively, athletically, spiritually, or otherwise. It's not selfish. It's essential.

18. **Unicorn Space (Player 2)** — [Same as Player 1 — both partners hold this card.]

19. **Values & Good Deeds** — Volunteering, charitable giving, teaching kids about empathy and service — this cardholder is the household's moral compass in action.

**Home**
20. **Car** — Oil changes, registration, insurance, repairs, car washes — this cardholder is the household's automotive manager. If it rolls, they own it.

22. **Dry Cleaning & Laundry** — Sorting, washing, drying, folding, hanging, dropping off, picking up — the complete laundry loop, from hamper to closet.

23. **Garbage & Recycling** — Taking out the trash, managing recycling, composting if applicable — and knowing which day is pickup day. Simple but perpetually undone if unowned.

24. **Groceries** — Menu planning, list-making, shopping (or ordering), and putting it all away. A massive weekly undertaking that underpins every meal.

26. **Home Maintenance & Repair** — Leaky faucets, broken hinges, burned-out bulbs — this cardholder either fixes it or hires someone who will. Proactive maintenance included.

27. **Home Purchase/Rental & Mortgage** — The lease, the mortgage, the refinancing — whoever holds this card is on top of the biggest financial transaction most families make.

28. **Hosting** — This cardholder owns the guest experience: inviting people over, planning the menu, cleaning up, and making sure everyone has a good time from arrival to departure.

29. **Laundry** — [Separate from dry cleaning — this is the everyday wash, fold, and put-away cycle for the household.]

30. **Lawn & Plants** — Mowing, watering, fertilizing, pruning — whether indoors or out, this cardholder keeps things green and alive.

36. **Moving** — The logistics of a household move: packing, hiring movers, updating addresses, transferring utilities. Exhausting by any measure.

37. **Packing & Unpacking (Kids; Local)** — Swim bag, sports gear, sleepover kit — this cardholder keeps kids properly equipped for wherever they're going.

38. **Packing & Unpacking (Travel)** — Packing for family trips — clothes, toiletries, gear, snacks — and unpacking and laundering on return.

39. **Tidying & Organizing** — The daily reset: picking up, putting away, making the home livable. Distinct from deep cleaning — this is the ongoing maintenance of order.

40. **Toilet & Bathroom Cleaning** — Scrubbing toilets, cleaning sinks, wiping mirrors — nobody's favorite task, but somebody has to own it.
