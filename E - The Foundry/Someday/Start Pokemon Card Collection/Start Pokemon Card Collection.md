# 🃏 Pokémon Card Collection

> A collection built for all the right reasons — nostalgia, investment, and something to share with Cyrus one day. Zero budget for now. Start with one card and trade up.

---

## Vision

- **Nostalgia** — reconnect with something that mattered as a kid
- **Investment** — build value over time through smart trading, not spending
- **Legacy** — a collection Cyrus can grow up with and inherit

**The philosophy:** Start with one card. Trade up, not out. Never buy when you can trade. Let patience do the work.

---

## The Trade-Up Strategy

Inspired by the "one red paperclip" model — start small, trade strategically upward in value over time.

1. Acquire a single entry-level card (can be cheap, $1–5, or gifted/found)
2. Research its trade value — find someone who wants it more than you
3. Trade for something worth slightly more
4. Repeat — each trade builds toward the cards that matter
5. Only spend money when a trade gap can't be bridged any other way

**Where to trade:**
- [ ] Research local card shops (LCS) in the East Bay
- [ ] Look into r/pkmntcgtrades (Reddit)
- [ ] Facebook Marketplace / local collector groups
- [ ] Card shows and conventions

## Collection Principles

- **Condition matters** — only keep cards in good condition or better; damaged cards are trade fodder
- **Focus over breadth** — a tight collection of meaningful cards beats a pile of random ones
- **Never pay retail for singles** — trade, negotiate, wait for deals
- **Sleeve everything immediately** — penny sleeves at minimum, top loaders for anything worth protecting
- **Document everything** — log every trade so you know your cost basis
