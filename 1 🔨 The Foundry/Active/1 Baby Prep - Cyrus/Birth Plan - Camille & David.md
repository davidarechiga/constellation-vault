## NEWBORN OPTIONS (EAST BAY STANDARD PRACTICE)

- ☑ **One full hour of uninterrupted skin-to-skin**

## ⚠️ CRITICAL: ADDRESSING BIAS & ENSURING EQUITABLE CARE

**As a Black woman, Camille faces 3-4x higher risk of maternal mortality and complications due to systemic racism and implicit bias in healthcare. We need the medical team to:**

1. **Take Camille's pain and concerns seriously**
   - If she reports severe pain, investigate immediately — do not dismiss it
   - Black women's pain is often underestimated; we need clinical assessment, not assumptions

2. **Speak directly to Camille (the patient), not around her**
   - She is the decision-maker about her body and her care
   - Direct all questions and explanations to her

3. **Watch for warning signs of preeclampsia, hemorrhage, and complications**
   - Black women have higher rates of these complications
   - Take symptoms seriously: severe headache, vision changes, excessive bleeding, chest pain, shortness of breath

4. **Use the BRAIN framework for all interventions**
   - Explain benefits, risks, alternatives, and what happens if we wait
   - Give us time to discuss decisions together
   - Respect our choices

5. **Provide trauma-informed, culturally respectful care**
   - We appreciate calm, respectful communication
   - Check in on emotional wellbeing, not just physical
   - Honor our cultural practices and birth preferences

6. **Postpartum vigilance**
   - Many Black maternal deaths occur after delivery
   - Please take all postpartum symptoms seriously (headache, high BP, bleeding, pain, swelling)

**David (partner) will advocate for Camille throughout labor. We appreciate your partnership in providing safe, respectful, evidence-based care.**
