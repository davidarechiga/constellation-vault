APGAR scores are a quick assessment done on newborns at 1 and 5 minutes after birth (sometimes 10 minutes if needed). It evaluates five things:

- **A**ppearance (skin color)
- **P**ulse (heart rate)
- **G**rimace (reflex response)
- **A**ctivity (muscle tone)
- **R**espiration (breathing)

Each category gets 0-2 points, for a total score of 0-10. A score of 7+ is normal, 4-6 means the baby may need some help, and below 4 indicates immediate medical attention is needed.
