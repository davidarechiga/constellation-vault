# video production checklist
### covers both pre and post-production stages

#templates

## **Pre-Production:**

### 1. **Concept and Planning:**
   - Define the purpose, message, and target audience of the video.
   - Brainstorm and develop the video concept, storyline, and script.
   - Create a storyboard or shot list to outline the visual elements.

### 2. **Budget and Schedule:**
   - Determine the budget for the project, including equipment, crew, locations, and post-production.
   - Create a production schedule with dates for shooting days, location scouting, and other essential tasks.

### 3. **Crew and Talent:**
   - Assemble the production team, including director, camera operators, sound recordists, and other relevant roles.
   - Cast actors or interviewees if needed and schedule their availability.

### 4. **Location Scouting:**
   - Scout and secure suitable shooting locations based on the script's requirements.
   - Obtain necessary permits or permissions for shooting at specific locations.

### 5. **Equipment and Gear:**
   - Gather and test all required cameras, lenses, lighting, audio equipment, and accessories.
   - Ensure backup equipment is available in case of technical issues.

### 6. **Production Design:**
   - Plan and create sets, props, costumes, and any necessary visual elements.
   - Ensure the visual design aligns with the intended message and aesthetics.

### 7. **Shot Planning:**
   - Finalize the shot list and camera angles for each scene.
   - Determine camera movements, framing, and composition.

## **Production:**

### 1. **Shooting:**
   - Capture footage according to the shot list and script.
   - Monitor lighting, sound quality, and camera settings during the shoot.
   - Take necessary notes for each take and scene.

### 2. **Audio Recording:**
   - Record high-quality audio using external microphones and proper recording techniques.
   - Monitor audio levels to prevent distortion or background noise.

### 3. **Directing and Communication:**
   - Guide actors and crew members to ensure the desired performance and visual elements are achieved.
   - Maintain effective communication among the crew to ensure a smooth production process.

### **Post-Production:**

1. **Footage Review and Logging:**
   - Review all captured footage and organize it by scenes and takes.
   - Create a log or spreadsheet to keep track of the best takes and notes.

### 2. **Editing:**
   - Import the selected footage into the editing software.
   - Arrange the clips according to the storyboard or script.
   - Edit the video for pacing, continuity, and visual appeal.

### 3. **Audio Post-Production:**
   - Clean up and enhance audio quality.
   - Add background music, sound effects, and voiceovers.

### 4. **Color Correction and Grading:**
   - Adjust color and tone to achieve the desired look and mood.
   - Ensure consistency in color across all scenes.

### 5. **Visual Effects and Graphics:**
   - Add any necessary visual effects, titles, subtitles, and graphics.
   - Ensure that visual elements enhance the storytelling.

### 6. **Review and Revisions:**
   - Review the edited video and make necessary revisions based on feedback.

### 7. **Final Export:**
   - Export the video in the desired format and resolution.
   - Test the exported video to ensure it plays correctly.

### 8. **Distribution and Promotion:**
   - Share the video on relevant platforms or channels.
   - Implement a promotional strategy to reach the target audience.

**Remember, this checklist can be adapted based on the specific needs of your project and the complexity of the production.**
