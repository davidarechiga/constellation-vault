## Training Philosophy (From Notes)

**Key Principles:**
- **More about engagement than perfection** - Energy and connection over rigid form
- **High energy sessions** - Keep it fun and engaging for River
- **Reset when attention wanes** - If River isn't paying attention, pause and re-engage
- **Less about trainer's perspective** - More about getting River's attention and maintaining it
- **Consistency over intensity** - Short, frequent sessions better than long infrequent ones

## Training Session Structure (15 min example)

**Warm-up (2 min):**
- Get River's attention
- Quick energy boost (play, exciting voice)
- Establish focus on you

**Core Training (10 min):**
- Practice 1-2 commands (heel, attention)
- 5-10 reps per command
- Treat rewards for success
- Break between exercises

**Cool-down (3 min):**
- Praise and affection
- Brief free sniffing time
- End on positive note

## Tips for Successful Training

✅ **DO:**
- Keep sessions short and fun (10-20 min max)
- High energy and positive tone
- Reward immediately when River succeeds
- End on positive note (successful command)
- Be consistent with commands and hand signals
- Train when River is alert (not right after eating)

❌ **DON'T:**
- Train when frustrated or tired
- Repeat failed command endlessly (reset and try again)
- Punish mistakes (positive reinforcement works better)
- Train in highly distracting environments (start at home)
- Expect perfection (focus on progress)

---

## Adaptation for Baby Arrival

**After baby comes:**
- Maintain shorter sessions (5-10 min)
- Focus on calm behavior around baby
- Train "settle" and "quiet" commands
- Continue basic obedience to keep River engaged
- Lower frequency to 2-3x/week if needed

**Goal:** Keep training habit alive through transition, even if intensity decreases.
