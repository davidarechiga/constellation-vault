> [!NOTE]
> 
> Makes about 6 waffles | Hands-on time: 30 minutes | From start to finish: 2 hours
> 
> Pearl sugar = compressed beet sugar (looks like rock salt). 
> 
> High melting point = stays whole while baking = crisp texture.

---
### Ingredients
- [ ] 1 1/4 tsp active dry yeast, or 1/2 tsp fresh yeast
- [ ] 1/4 cup (60 ml / 60 g) lukewarm water (between 90°F and 100°F / 32°C and 38°C)
- [ ] 1/2 cup (4 oz / 113 g) unsalted butter
- [ ] 3/4 cup (3.7 oz / 105 g) pastry flour
- [ ] 1 1/4 cups (6.3 oz / 180 g) all-purpose flour
- [ ] 1 tbsp granulated sugar, plus more for sprinkling
- [ ] 1/2 tsp Maldon sea salt
- [ ] 5 eggs (8.75 oz / 250 g), room temp
- [ ] 1 vanilla bean
- [ ] 3 tbsp pearl sugar (see note)

---
#### Substitutions
• 1 tsp vanilla extract = 1 vanilla bean
• All-purpose flour can replace pastry flour

---
### Instructions
1. Combine yeast + water in small bowl. Let sit 5 min.
2. Melt butter. Cool to 115°F (46°C).
3. In large bowl, mix flours + granulated sugar + salt.
4. In medium bowl, crack eggs. Split vanilla bean, scrape pulp into eggs. Whisk well.
5. Add egg mix to flour mix, then add yeast mix + melted butter. Whisk until smooth.
6. Cover, let rise until doubled (1 hr) or refrigerate overnight.
7. Add pearl sugar. Let dough rest 15 min.
8. Preheat waffle maker (Belgian-style) to medium-high.
9. Scoop 1/2 to 3/4 cup (120–150 ml) batter into waffle maker. Sprinkle granulated sugar on top.
10. Cook until indicator light goes off or waffle is browned + crisp. Serve immediately.
