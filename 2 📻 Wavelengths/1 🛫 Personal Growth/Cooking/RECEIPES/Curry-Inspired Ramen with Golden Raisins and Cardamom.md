
*Prep Time: 1 hour | Cook Time: 3-4 hours | Serves: 4*

Recipe for [[Curry Marinated Eggs]]
## Ingredients

### For the Curry Broth
- [x] 8 cups high-quality chicken or vegetable stock
- [ ] 2-3 green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
- [x] 2 tablespoons yellow curry powder
- [ ] 1 tablespoon garam masala
- [x] 2-inch piece ginger, minced
- [x] 4 garlic cloves, minced
- [x] 1 small onion, finely diced
- [x] 1 tablespoon tomato paste
- [x] 1 can (14oz) coconut milk
- [x] 1 tablespoon red miso
- [x] 2 tablespoons neutral oil
- [ ] Salt to taste

### For the Curry-Infused Chashu
- [x] 2 lbs pork belly, skin-on
- [x] 1 cup soy sauce
- [x] 1 cup mirin
- [x] 1/2 cup sake
- [x] 2 green cardamom pods, crushed
- [x] 1 tablespoon curry powder
- [x] 2 star anise
- [x] 1-inch ginger, sliced
- [x] 2 garlic cloves, crushed
- [x] Butcher's twine for rolling

### For the Raisin Garnish
- 1/4 cup golden raisins
- 1 cup hot dashi or water for soaking

### Additional Components
- 4 portions fresh ramen noodles
- 4 soft-boiled eggs
- 2 cups bean sprouts, blanched
- 4 sheets nori
- 4 scallions, thinly sliced
- 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
- 4 small pats of butter
- Curry chili oil for serving (optional)

## Instructions

### Prepare the Chashu (Start 1 Day Ahead)
1. Score the skin of the pork belly in a crosshatch pattern. Roll and tie with butcher's twine.
2. Combine all marinade ingredients in a large zip-top bag or container.
3. Add pork belly and marinate overnight (12-24 hours).
4. Preheat oven to 275°F (135°C).
5. Remove pork from marinade, pat dry. Strain and reserve marinade.
6. Heat a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear pork on all sides until golden.
7. Add strained marinade, bring to simmer.
8. Cover and transfer to oven. Cook 2.5-3 hours until tender.
9. Let cool in liquid, then refrigerate until firm for easy slicing.

### Prepare the Curry Broth
1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat.
2. Add onion, ginger, and garlic. Sauté until softened (5-7 minutes).
3. Add curry powder and garam masala. Toast for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
4. Add tomato paste, cook for 1 minute.
5. Add stock and cardamom pods. Bring to simmer.
6. Simmer gently for 1 hour, skimming as needed.
7. Whisk in miso until dissolved.
8. Add coconut milk, simmer 10 minutes more.
9. Strain broth, taste and adjust seasoning.

### Prepare Toppings
1. Soak golden raisins in hot dashi for 30 minutes, drain.
2. Blanch bean sprouts in boiling water for 1 minute, shock in ice water.
3. Cook eggs: 6.5 minutes in boiling water, then ice bath. Peel and halve.
4. Slice scallions thinly.
5. Warm corn if using frozen.
6. Slice chilled chashu thinly.

### Assembly
1. Heat curry broth to a simmer.
2. Cook ramen noodles according to package instructions.
3. Divide noodles among four bowls.
4. Ladle hot broth over noodles.
5. Arrange toppings: 
   - Sliced chashu
   - Half egg
   - Bean sprouts
   - Corn
   - 4-5 golden raisins per bowl
   - Scallions
   - Nori
   - Small pat of butter
6. Serve immediately with curry chili oil on the side.

## Notes
- Remove cardamom pods before serving broth
- Chashu can be made up to 3 days ahead
- Broth can be made 1 day ahead and reheated
- Adjust curry powder and spices to taste
- For spicier ramen, add cayenne or chili oil
