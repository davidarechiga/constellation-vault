## <span style="font-size:17pt;"><b>13.2 Social Perception and Cognition</b></span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>Social Perception and Cognition</b></span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">the processes for learning about others and making inferences from that information</span>
### <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>First Impressions</b></span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>The Primary Effect</b></span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">the information we learn about someone influences us more than later information does</span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;">first impressions can become self-fulfilling prophecies</span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">expectations that increase the probability of the predicted event</span>
### <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>Stereotypes and Prejudices</b></span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>Stereo Type</b></span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">a belief or expectation about a group of people</span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>prejudice</b></span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">an unfavorable attitude toward a group of people</span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>Discrimination</b></span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">unequal treatment of different groups</span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>Implicit Measures of Prejudice</b></span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">implicit association test</span>
		- <span style="font-size:14pt;">measures reactions to combinations of categories</span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>Overcoming Prejudice</b></span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">interaction reduces prejudice</span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">get groups to work tougher on a common goal</span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">competition breeds hostility</span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">cooperation leads to friendships</span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>Decreasing Prejudice by Increasing Acceptance</b></span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">We’re all the same approach breeds prejudice</span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">multiculturalism</span>
		- <span style="font-size:14pt;">accepting, recognizing and enjoying the differences around people and groups and the unique contributions that each person can offer</span>
### <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>Attribution</b></span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;">the set of thought processes we use to assign causes to our own behavior and that of others</span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>Internal Versus External Causes</b></span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">internal attributions (dispositional)</span>
		- <span style="font-size:14pt;">explanations based on someone’s attitude, personality traits, abilities, or other characteristics</span>
		- <span style="font-size:14pt;">example: Your brother walked to work this morning because he likes the exercise</span>
		- <span style="font-size:14pt;">unfamiliar culture may be different than yours and often gets attributed to personality</span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">external attributions (Situational)</span>
		- <span style="font-size:14pt;">explanations based on the situation incising events that would influence almost anyone</span>
		- <span style="font-size:14pt;">example: he walked because his car wouldn’t start</span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">3 types of information influence</span>
		- <span style="font-size:14pt;">Consensus Information</span>
			- <span style="font-size:14pt;">how the person’s behavior compares with other peoples behavior</span>
		- <span style="font-size:14pt;">Consistency Information</span>
			- <span style="font-size:14pt;">how the person’s behavior varies from one time to the next</span>
		- <span style="font-size:14pt;">Distinctiveness</span>
			- <span style="font-size:14pt;">how the person’s behavior varies from one situation to another</span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>The Actor-Observer Effect</b></span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">People are more likely to make internal attributions for other people’s behavoir and more likely to make external attributions for their own</span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>The Fundamental Attribution Error (correspondence bias)</b></span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">To make internal attributions for people’s behavior even when we see evidence for an external influence on behavior</span>
- <span style="font-size:14pt;"><b>Cultural Différentes in Attribution and Related Matters</b></span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">Western Cultures make more internal attributions</span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">eastern is more external</span> 
- <span style="font-size:14pt;">Using Attributions to Manage Perceptions of Ourselves</span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">SelfServing Bias</span>
		- <span style="font-size:14pt;">attributions that we adopt to maximize credit for success and minimize blame for failure</span>
			- <span style="font-size:14pt;">most Americans rate themselves above average on almost everything</span>
			- <span style="font-size:14pt;">not a prominent in Asians</span>
	- <span style="font-size:14pt;">Self Handicapping strategies</span>
		- <span style="font-size:14pt;">intentionally put themselves at a disadvantage to provide an excuse for failure</span>
