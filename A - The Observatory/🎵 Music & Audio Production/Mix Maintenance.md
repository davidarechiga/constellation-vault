# Mix Maintenance

> All of these tasks are **non-critical listening work** — no fresh ears or peak focus required. Grind through them mechanically. This is mix prep, not mixing. Skip it and you'll pay for it later with pumping, mud, and phase issues.

---

## Session Housekeeping

- [ ] **Rename all tracks** — "Audio 47" vs "Snare Top" is the difference between flow and frustration at 2am
- [ ] **Trim silence / clean up regions** — removes CPU load and stops dead air from triggering gates/compressors unexpectedly
- [ ] **Bounce virtual instruments to audio** — frees CPU, locks in the sound, and gives you a real waveform to edit
- [ ] **Delete duplicate and unused tracks** — old muted versions and disabled channels slow down the session and clutter the handoff
- [ ] **Check sample rate consistency** — Splice samples may be 44.1k in a 48k session; verify nothing sounds pitchy or off-tempo

---

## Infrastructure & Routing

- [ ] **Set up reverb/delay sends** — build the mix into a real space from the start; don't retrofit it later
- [ ] **Set up parallel compression busses** — NY compression or parallel drum crush already routed means you just bring up a fader when needed
- [ ] **Set up busses/stems** — drum bus, bass bus, instrument groups; gives macro control and makes handoff easier
- [ ] **Color code and group tracks** — kick/snare/hats one color, bass another, synths another; takes 10 min, saves hours

---

## Gain & Levels

- [ ] **Gain stage every track** — set input gain so nothing peaks over -6dBFS before any plugins; single biggest gift before handoff
- [ ] **Rough gain automation passes** — pull down obviously too-loud hits or accidental loud regions; prevents limiter disasters
- [ ] **Check mono compatibility on low end** — sub bass should be mono; if it's wide it'll disappear on phones and some clubs

---

## Drum Work

- [ ] **Gate the drums** — even rough thresholds now means bleed isn't clouding decisions while you work
- [ ] **Clean up bad bleed** — especially kick/snare/tom bleed; adds up fast in the 200–400Hz range (known mud zone)
- [ ] **Time align drums** — phase between close mics and overheads makes or breaks how a kit hits; non-negotiable before any EQ

---

## Performance Editing

- [ ] **Comp all takes** — pick the best bits of every instrument; don't mix a placeholder performance
- [ ] **Crossfades everywhere** — hard edits cause clicks/pops that trick you into bad EQ decisions

---

## Session Organization

- [ ] **Set tempo and drop markers** — lock the tempo, drop markers at every section (intro, verse, drop, break, outro); your collaborator shouldn't have to guess
- [ ] **Note plugin dependencies** — list every plugin used so your collaborator knows what they need before they open the session

---

## Handoff Notes

- [ ] **Write notes for your collaborator** — vibe, references (Joy Orbison, Jamie XX, etc.), what you want the drop to feel like, what you're unsure about; more context = better decisions with fewer interruptions
