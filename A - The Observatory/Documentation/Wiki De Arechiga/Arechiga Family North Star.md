## 🌟 Who We Are (Timeless Identity)

We are **David and Camille Arechiga** — a creative, intellectually curious family rooted in intentionality, equity, and joy. We build a home that is warm and organized. We make time for what matters and say no to what doesn't. We are becoming the kind of family we wish we'd grown up around.

### Our Core Values

| Value | What It Means for Us |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Equity** | Fair distribution of cognitive and physical labor. We use Fair Play as a living system — both partners hold ownership with full CPE (Conception, Planning, Execution). |
| **Intentionality** | We don't drift. We make deliberate choices about how we spend time, money, and energy. |
| **Connection** | Our relationship is the foundation everything else is built on. We protect it — especially when life is loud. |
| **Creative Expression** | David and Camille each have Unicorn Space — protected time for the things that make us who we are outside of parenting and partnership. |
| **Growth** | We invest in therapy, learning, and honest reflection. We say hard things with care. |
| **Financial Stability** | We live within our means, plan ahead, and build a buffer so decisions are made from calm, not crisis. |

## 🧭 Decision-Making Framework

> *This section is written for both human reflection and Claude skill reasoning. When Claude is helping with scheduling, prioritization, or task triage — apply this framework top-down.*

---

### Layer 1 — Non-Negotiables (Never Trade Away)

These are the floor. No goal, project, or opportunity overrides them.

| Non-Negotiable | Why It's Locked |
|----------------|-----------------|
| **Cyrus's care and safety** | He is fully dependent. Nothing else goes first. |
| **Camille's postpartum recovery** | Recovery is finite — compromise it now and pay later. This phase is Q1–Q2 2026. |
| **Sleep (David and Camille)** | Sleep deprivation compounds every other problem. Protect it structurally, not just in theory. |
| **Fair Play minimum standards** | If the household minimum standards of care are slipping, all other goals are downstream noise. Check [[Fair Play Accountability]] before adding anything new. |
| **Couple check-in (daily, non-logistical)** | The relationship is the operating system. If it degrades, everything else runs slower. |

**Claude rule:** If a proposed task, event, or commitment conflicts with a non-negotiable — flag it explicitly. Don't silently schedule over it.

---

### Layer 2 — Priority Stack (When Things Compete)

When two legitimate things conflict, use this ordering:

```
1. Family health & safety (Cyrus, Camille's recovery, David's mental health)
2. Shared quarterly priorities (see Q2 focus above)
3. Relationship investment (date nights, connection rituals)
4. Income-generating or deadline-driven work
5. Annual intentions (Clear Mind, Healthy Body, etc.)
6. Unicorn Space (creative work, personal projects)
7. Community & social obligations
8. Aspirational / someday items
```

**Claude rule:** When building a daily schedule or morning brief, load items from the top of the stack first. Unicorn Space and social items only appear if layers 1–5 have breathing room that day.

---

### Layer 3 — Energy Accounting

Every day has a finite energy budget. Not all hours are equal.

| Time of Day | David's Energy Profile | Best For |
|-------------|------------------------|----------|
| **Morning (before 10am)** | High focus, clear head | Deep work, financial tasks, writing |
| **Midday (10am–2pm)** | Active, social | Calls, errands, childcare presence, admin |
| **Afternoon (2pm–6pm)** | Moderate, dipping | Light tasks, household cards, creative warm-up |
| **Evening (after 7pm)** | Low cognition, high creative | [[frmwrk_]] radio/music, reading, connecting with Camille |

**Postpartum modifier (active through ~Q2 2026):** All energy estimates are reduced by ~30%. Sleep interruptions are real. Schedule lighter than feels necessary.

**Claude rule:** When placing tasks on a schedule, match task type to energy window. Never stack two deep-work blocks back to back. Flag if the day looks overloaded before confirming it.

---

### Layer 4 — The Equity Check

Before finalizing any plan that affects both David and Camille — ask:

1. **Who carries the cognitive load here?** (Not just the doing — the thinking, planning, remembering)
2. **Is one person's Unicorn Space being consistently sacrificed for the other's?**
3. **Is Camille being asked to add anything during her recovery/dissertation crunch?**
4. **Does this plan require a conversation with Camille first — or can David execute independently?**

**Claude rule:** If a plan requires Camille's agreement or affects her time, surface it as a *discussion item* in the evening note or morning brief — not a scheduled event. Don't put things on the shared calendar that haven't been negotiated.

---

### Layer 5 — The Vibe Check (Final Gate)

Before committing to something — especially optional commitments:

- **Does this excite at least one of us?** (Not just "it's probably fine")
- **Would we say yes to this on a well-rested Tuesday?** (If only yes when exhausted or pressured — that's a no)
- **Is this adding to our life or filling a void?** (Avoid saying yes out of guilt, obligation, or fear of missing out)

**Claude rule:** If scheduling something that feels like an obligation rather than an intention, add a note: *"Confirm this is a genuine yes before blocking calendar time."*

## 🤖 Claude Skill Instructions

> *How the Claude skills in this vault should use this document.*

### `/morning` skill
1. Read the **Q2 Quarterly Focus** — surface any priority with an imminent deadline
2. Check the **Non-Negotiables** — flag if any MSC (minimum standards of care) are at risk today
3. Apply the **Priority Stack** when selecting Top 3 items for the day
4. Use **Energy Accounting** to place tasks in the right time blocks
5. If Camille's schedule or needs are involved — treat as a discussion item, not a decision

### `/evening` skill
1. Review what was completed today against the **Priority Stack**
2. Apply the **Equity Check** when seeding tomorrow's plan — ask who carried the load today
3. Surface any **non-negotiable** that was missed today — name it explicitly in Prep For Tomorrow
4. Seed tomorrow's Top 3 from the quarterly focus, not just open tasks

### `/weekly` skill
1. Grade the week against each **Life Area** — did the important ones get attention?
2. Check progress on **Q2 Quarterly Priorities** — are they moving or stalling?
3. Apply the **Equity Check** at the week level — who carried more cognitive load this week?
4. Surface any annual intention that hasn't been touched in 2+ weeks
5. Flag if Unicorn Space for either partner was skipped this week

### `/organize-inbox` skill
When a note doesn't have an obvious home, classify it using the **Life Areas** list above. If a note touches a **Non-Negotiable** (Cyrus, Camille, household MSC), route it to an active project rather than Atlas archive.

### General task triage
When deciding whether to surface a task:
- **High priority signal words**: deadline, childcare, Camille, Cyrus, bills, health, therapy
- **Deprioritize signal words**: someday, maybe, idea, explore, eventually
- **Needs discussion flag**: anything that affects Camille's time, schedule, or energy
