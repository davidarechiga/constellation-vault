**Makes:** ~1 lb 10¾ oz

## Ingredients

- [ ] ¼ oz chile morita, destemmed
- [ ] ¾ oz garlic, roughly chopped
- [ ] 5¼ oz white onion, roughly chopped
- [ ] ⅜ oz chile serrano, sliced in half lengthwise and deseeded
- [ ] 7 oz tomatillo, husks discarded
- [ ] 1 lb 2¾ oz plum tomatoes, halved, stems removed
- [ ] Salt to taste
- [ ] ½ oz cilantro

-----

## Instructions

1. Place a comal or cast-iron skillet over **high heat**. Char the dried chiles and garlic. Cook chiles ~2–3 min until skin softens slightly, then transfer to warm water to rehydrate for **10–15 min**. Leave garlic on heat and add onion.
2. After ~3–4 min, move garlic to molcajete or blender. Add fresh serrano (cut side down), whole tomatillos, and tomatoes (flesh side down). Flip serranos to char both sides. Roast everything ~10–15 min, flipping occasionally, until charred and cooked through. Turn off heat, leave veggies in place.
3. **Blender method:** Add all roasted ingredients, salt, and cilantro. Pulse to desired consistency.
4. **Molcajete method:** Grind onions, 1 tomatillo, and garlic into a rough paste. Add remaining tomatillos, tomatoes, and salt. Grind until mostly smooth (remove large tomato skin chunks).
5. Add softened rehydrated chiles. Continue grinding or blending to desired consistency. Stir in fresh cilantro without mashing.
6. Serve immediately. Keeps refrigerated, covered, for **up to 4 days**.
