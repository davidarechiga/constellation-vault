# Build Around Ingredients

A flexible cooking framework: start with a **freezer protein** as the anchor, grab one key ingredient, and let it determine the meal. Reduces decision fatigue and prevents over-buying.

---

## The Framework

**Thaw a protein the night before** — this is the most important step. Everything else builds around it.

---

## If you find...

### Pasta or Noodles
Protein + garlic + whatever veg + olive oil or jarred sauce.
Done in 25 minutes.
> *Examples: pasta pomodoro with sausage, garlic noodles with chicken, spaghetti aglio e olio with shrimp*

### Canned Tomatoes or Tomato Sauce
- **Lunch:** Shakshuka — eggs poached in spiced tomato sauce. Very low effort.
- **Dinner:** Quick protein braise — sear protein, add tomatoes + spices, simmer.

### Bread or Tortillas
Protein tacos or a loaded sandwich. Good for a lazy Sunday lunch.

> *Examples: al pastor-style chicken tacos, pulled pork sandwich, steak torta*

### Soup Base or Broth
Protein + whatever veg + noodles or rice = homemade soup.

> *Examples: chicken noodle, beef and rice, pho-style broth with whatever protein*

### Rice or Grains
Rice bowl — protein on top, pickled anything, maybe a fried egg.

> *Examples: Korean-style rice bowl, burrito bowl, grain salad with chicken*

### Eggs (always in fridge)
- Fried rice with leftover protein
- Breakfast-for-dinner with whatever meat you have
- Frittata — protein + veg + eggs, finish in the oven

---

## Pantry Anchors to Always Have
- Garlic
- Olive oil
- Soy sauce
- Canned tomatoes
- Dried pasta or rice
- Broth (chicken or beef)
- Hot sauce
