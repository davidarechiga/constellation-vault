# Weekly Gym Routine

Full Program · Sets · Reps · Tempo · RPE · Progression · Rest Timers

-----

## How to Read This Routine

- **Tempo** = Eccentric / Pause / Concentric (e.g. 3-1-1 = 3 sec down, 1 sec pause, 1 sec up)
- **RPE** = Rate of Perceived Exertion out of 10 (RPE 8 = hard but ~2 reps left in the tank)
- **Rest** = Start your Apple Watch timer the moment you rack the weight
- **Progressive Overload** = Add 2.5–5 lbs OR 1–2 reps per week when top of range feels clean
- Weeks 1–2: Learn movements, RPE 6–7 | Weeks 3–4: Add weight | Weeks 5–6: Add a set | Weeks 7–8: Deload (–40% vol)

## Apple Watch Timing

- Open Workout app > Other > use the built-in timer, OR use the Timer app from your watch face
- Add a Timer complication to your watch face for one-tap access between sets
- 45s = isolation finishers (calves, curls) | 60s = accessory work | 75s = moderate compounds | 90s = heavy compounds | 2–3 min = max effort sets
- Rule of thumb: If you can start your next set before the timer goes off, you went too light

**How it works:** Do one set of each exercise back to back with no rest between = 1 round. Rest 60s after completing all 5 exercises, then repeat for 3 rounds total.

- **Exception:** Hollow Body Hold is only 2 rounds — skip it on round 3.

> ⏱ **Rest Timers:** Between exercises: No rest · Between rounds: 60s · Hollow Body Hold: 2 rounds only

## Progressive Overload Plan

|Phase    |Focus                                                                      |
|---------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|Weeks 1–2|Learn movements. Hit middle of rep ranges. RPE 6–7. Focus on form over load|
|Weeks 3–4|Add 2.5–5 lbs on compound lifts where top of range felt clean and easy     |
|Weeks 5–6|Add a set to main compound lifts (5 sets instead of 4)                     |
|Weeks 7–8|Deload — drop volume by 40%, keep same weight. Let joints and CNS recover  |
|Reset    |Start the cycle again with heavier baseline weights                        |

## Nutrition Anchors

- **Protein:** ~0.7–1g per lb of bodyweight daily
- **Pre-workout:** Carbs + protein 60–90 min before (oatmeal, banana, Greek yogurt)
- **Post-workout:** Protein within 60 min of finishing (shake, eggs, chicken)
- **Hydration:** At least 80–100 oz water on training days

## Recovery Notes — Newborn Mode

- Don’t train to failure on high sleep-debt days — drop RPE target to 6 across the board
- Saturday functional day can swap to full rest if the week was rough — no guilt
- Match cardio intensity to energy — walk instead of intervals when running on 3 hours of sleep
- Recovery matters more than any individual session right now — consistency over intensity
- Use Apple Watch HR to keep honest: if resting HR is 10+ bpm above normal, take the day easyFf
