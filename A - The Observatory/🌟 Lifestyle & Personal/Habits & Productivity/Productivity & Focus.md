# Productivity & Focus

A collection of principles for staying focused, setting priorities, and getting meaningful work done — without overthinking the system.

---

## Priorities

> A priority is a thing regarded as more important than another.

The hard part isn't knowing what your priorities are — it's *acting* on them when everything feels urgent.

**Core principles:**
- Make a list of priorities because life is hard and your brain can't hold everything
- Focus on priorities — if you get lost, remember it's about the work, not the reward
- If you focus on important tasks a majority of the time, you'll be getting things done. If you focus on small stuff but not the big stuff, you'll feel busy but unproductive

### Gap vs. Gain Thinking

- **Gap thinking:** Measuring yourself against an ideal you haven't reached → leads to frustration
- **Gain thinking:** Measuring yourself against where you started → reveals progress

Journaling is one of the best tools for Gain thinking — it forces you to see how far you've come rather than how far you have left to go.

## On Discipline & Organization

> It's important to find a reason to be disciplined.

Discipline without purpose is just rigidity. The goal isn't to be organized for its own sake — it's to reduce friction between you and the things that matter.

**Minimal is the goal.** Not minimal stuff — minimal friction. The fewer decisions between you and doing the work, the better.

### The Organized vs. Messy Spectrum

You don't need a perfect system. You need a system that:
1. Captures things so you don't forget
2. Surfaces what matters today
3. Gets out of your way the rest of the time

If your system requires more maintenance than the work it supports, it's too complex.

## Automation & Systems

Small automations compound over time:
- Add reminders to calendar instead of trying to remember
- Use templates for repeating workflows (daily notes, project setup)
- Batch similar tasks (email, errands, admin) instead of switching all day
- Set up environment cues that make the right action the default (see [[habits#Environment Design]])

> The best productivity system is the one you actually use.

## Getting Things Done (Practical Framework)

From [[zen habits]] — Leo Babauta's simplified GSD program:

1. **Morning session** (5-10 min) — Pick 3 important tasks + 3 admin tasks for today
2. **Focus sessions** — Work on one task for 15-25 minutes, no switching. Take a break. Repeat
3. **Uncertainty meditation** — When you feel like procrastinating, pause and sit with the discomfort instead of running from it
4. **Evening review** (10 min) — What worked? What got in the way? How to adjust?

### The 10 Obstacles to Getting Stuff Done

1. Putting off starting (discomfort)
2. Switching between tasks (distraction)
3. Perfectionism (waiting for ideal conditions)
4. Other people (delays, friction)
5. Distractions & interruptions
6. Being tired / low energy
7. Fear, uncertainty, feeling overwhelmed

**The common fix:** Make the start as small as possible. Create conditions that make starting easier. Practice starting over and over until it's automatic.

## Quick Reference

**When you're stuck:**
- What's the *one* most important thing right now?
- What's the smallest possible first step?
- Am I avoiding this because it's hard, or because it doesn't actually matter?

**When you're overwhelmed:**
- Write everything down (get it out of your head)
- Pick three things for today
- Do the first one for 15 minutes

**When you're procrastinating:**
- Notice you're in discomfort — that's normal
- Sit with it for 30 seconds instead of switching tasks
- Start the two-minute version (see [[habits#The Two-Minute Rule]])
