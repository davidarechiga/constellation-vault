Therapy Session: Self-Perception, Control, and Core Beliefs

11:03 - 11:54 GMT-07:00

David is actively addressing household equity and personal time, while exploring how his need for control and perfectionism stems from deeper core beliefs and self-perception.

Key Points

Personal Updates and Household Management: David shared recent personal developments, including a new approach to household responsibilities.
  *  David and Camille implemented a 'Fair Play' system using Eve Rodsky's cards to equally distribute household chores, addressing a long-standing request from Camille.
  *  David initiated a weekly radio show, securing 6 hours per week for personal pursuits, a time allocation agreed upon with Camille.

Anxiety and Perception of Control: David explored the roots of his anxiety, linking it to a need for control.
  *  David experiences significant anxiety when showing up late, attributing it to his perception of how others view him.
  *  He noted a dichotomy where he judges himself for lateness but not others, highlighting a lack of self-kindness.
  *  The therapist suggested that David's need for rigidity and control, such as punctuality, might be a compensatory strategy for perceived lack of control in other life areas, like his speech impediment.

Core Beliefs and Perfectionism: The discussion delved into how core beliefs drive perfectionism and self-judgment.
  *  The therapist explained that negative core beliefs, often developed early in life, can lead to shame and a feeling of being 'broken' or 'defective'.
  *  Perfectionism can serve as a compensatory strategy, driven by conditional 'if-then' statements, such as 'If I am perfect, then people will like me' or 'If I am perfect, then I can prove I am competent'.
  *  David's rigidity in scheduling and meeting management, like running company all-hands meetings as a 'live show', provides a sense of self-confidence and control.

Flexibility and Self-Compassion: The conversation emphasized the importance of flexibility and self-compassion for well-being.
  *  Rigidity in thinking has a high cost, leading to perfectionism and 'should' statements, overestimating negative effects of not adhering to strict rules.
  *  The therapist used the metaphor of bamboo bending in a storm to illustrate the value of flexibility and adaptability in life.
  *  Self-compassion involves treating oneself with dignity and respect, not as something to be earned, but as an intrinsic right.

Actionable Insights and Next Steps: The session concluded with practical advice and a task for David to reflect on.
  *  David acknowledged that his rigidity, initially perceived as helpful, might be a compensation for insecurities and causes unnecessary suffering.
  *  The therapist provided a PDF on core beliefs and asked David to review it before the next appointment, identifying his own core beliefs and their origins.
